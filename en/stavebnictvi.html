BIM

Building Information Modeling (BIM) is our key method for processing a digital/informational model of a building. Its foundation is a 3D model, which contains information and parameters of all its included elements. It has significant advantages in the processing project documentation phase. It allows us to work with this data in subsequent phases of the project – not only in construction and operation but also in liquidation. This is one of the future standards of construction. The whole group of SUDOP companies have been participating for many years in pilot projects and active participation in the standardization of BIM in the Czech Republic.

Digital twin

The digital twin is a set of data and information systems, thanks to which we can provide integrated work with this data. It is a virtual image of a real building, which we can predictively manage with IT tools. It can consist of a standalone building, a transport road, or even a territorial unit of a region or city with all the real estate. The digital twin’s environment ensures, among other things automatic data collection (e.g. from building information models), their management (distributing information between individual information systems and their updates) or their transfer to third parties (e.g. using data for a digital technical map or the digitalization of construction management).

D35 – Staré Město – Mohelnice

Authority: Directorate of Roads and Highways Czech Republic

D35 – Staré Město – Mohelnice

The subject of this documentation is the specifications of the technical solutions for the roadway D35 in the section between Staré Město and Mohelnice, including the connection of the town of Mohelnice to the D35. The project was designated by the contracting authority as a pilot BIM on the level of a study to find the optimal detail of the BIM processing method, which will meet all the requirements imposed by the contracting authority at this stage of project documentation in connection with the technological possibilities.

D3 0310/II Hodějovice – Třebonín

Authority: Directorate of Roads and Highways Czech Republic

D3 0310/II Hodějovice – Třebonín

The aim of this pilot project was to verify the use of the BIM method for transport structures in the documentation phase for a building permit (DSP). The assignment of the project was solved as the so-called reverse, which was based on the documentation that has been already processed by the provider in the DSP stage. As in the previous study, it was primarily a matter of testing SW tools and procedures for processing the digital information model, including its graphical and non-graphical information. The assignment also included construction progress simulation of a motorway over the river Malše.

Processor: PUDIS

Processor: SUDOP PRAHA

Processor: BIM Consulting

Processor: cadconsulting

I/42 Brno VMO Žabovřeská I – 1st phase

Authority: Directorate of Roads and Highways Czech Republic

I/42 Brno VMO Žabovřeská I – 1st phase

The aim of this very complex pilot project was to examine the possibility of BIM design for urban transport structures with a wide range of diverse buildings types. As with the project mentioned above, it was a so-called reverse assignment. The digital information model was developed at the level of detail of the project documentation for the construction. In addition to a number of buildings beginning with the preparation of the building and ending with vegetation modifications, geodetic and geological models were also solved. For better communication between the customer and the processor, a common data environment (CDE) ProjectWise from the American company Bentley was used.

Processor: METROPROJEKT Praha

Processor: cadconsulting

Processor: BIM Consulting

Subway section I.D1 on the D Line of the Prague metro

Authority: Prague Public Transit Company

Subway section I.D1 on the D Line of the Prague metro

The subject of this project is the project documentation of the operational sector I.D1 on the D line of the Prague metro. This first operational sector I.D1 of the D line, which will contain five stations including the rail connection to the existing C line at the Pankrác C line station, is the foundational phase of construction of the new Prague metro line. In 2018 project work was focused on the first two stations of this section – the Pankrác D station and Olbrachtova station– e.g. section ID1a. In 2019 work commenced on the section ID1b, including the stations Nádraží Krč, Nemocnice Krč and Nové Dvory. In the context of the project, BIM was primarily used for the creation of 2D project documentation, 3D spatial documentation and 5D compilation of the project budget.

Processor: METROPROJEKT Praha

ČSOB Headquarters

Authority: ČSOB

ČSOB Headquarters

The ČSOB – SHQ headquarters is a modern administrative building for 1400 employees and is connected via technological systems and pedestrian bridges, and the original NHQ building. The project documentation of all professions was conducted in the BIM method, which was completely unprecedented at the time in scope, detail, and integrity, and which was awarded the title of Building of the Year 2019 in the BIM category. The quality of the building was confirmed by the prestigious LEED platinum-level certification when it received 90 total points and ranks among the 10 best-rated buildings in the European Union.
